# DesignTek 101 — Design a News Sorter

**A 30-minute challenge. One screen. Your rules.**

---

## The Premise

Building a working RSS reader with manual categorization is genuinely complex. Async data fetching, XML parsing, state management, persistent sorting logic. The kind of thing that would take a developer days to scaffold from scratch.

With Claude, you can describe the interaction you want in design language and get working code in minutes. The complexity is real — which is exactly what makes the payoff feel earned.

---

## Your Challenge

Build a single-screen app that pulls design news from a real RSS feed and lets you sort through it manually. Every decision about how that sorting works is yours to make.

---

## The Only Rules

1. The app must read from at least one real design RSS feed. UX Collective, Muzli, Smashing Magazine, or any source you choose.
2. Articles must be sortable into at least two categories. You define what those categories mean.
3. The sorting must be manual. You decide the fate of each article, one at a time.
4. The interface must feel intentional and yours. No default browser styles — unless that is a deliberate choice.

---

## Everything Else Is Open

- **How you sort:** drag, swipe, button, keyboard shortcut, long press, gesture.
- **What your categories mean.**
- **What information you surface** from each article — and what you hide.
- **How sorted items are displayed** afterward.
- **What the motion and transitions feel like.**
- **What the overall visual language is.**

The only wrong answer is an interface that does not reflect a deliberate point of view.

---

## Before You Start

Take two minutes to answer these questions out loud before writing a single prompt to Claude:

1. What are my two categories, and why do they make sense together?
2. What does sorting *feel* like in my interface? What is the physical metaphor?
3. What one word describes the visual feeling I am going for?

Your answers to those three questions are your brief. Build toward them.
